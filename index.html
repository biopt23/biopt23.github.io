The 1 st workshop on
Bilevel Optimization for Machine Learning (BIOPT)

Thursday Dec. 14th 2023, New Orleans
In-person (Ballroom XX) and Virtual


Bilevel optimization is a growing field of research that has shown promising results in various practical applications in the ML field. Around 40 papers mention bilevel optimization in NeurIPS 2022. It consists in optimizing a function whose value depends on the result of another optimization problem. The implicit definition of the function that needs to be optimized makes the problem more complex to solve than regular single level optimization. Starting from gradient based hyper-parameter optimization, bilevel optimization has however been applied successfully to automatically select data augmentation strategies or neural architectures, solve meta learning problems, or to derive implicit deep learning architectures that allow to train very deep networks with memory efficient procedures. In the recent years, a large variety of solvers have been proposed to tackle such problems in a rigorous fashion for strongly convex inner objectives. However, most applied fields have not benefit from this optimization research and have developed their own solvers, often relying on heuristic strategies with no convergence guarantees.

This workshop aims to bring together researchers and practitioners working on bilevel optimization to foster collaboration and share recent advances in the field. It will aim to bridge two communities that seldom interact: the optimization community which develops and analyzes hypergradient computations and efficient solvers for bilevel problems, and the researchers who develop solvers for practical bilevel problems in ML, in particular using deep equilibrium networks (DEQs), automatic data augmentation, neural architecture search, meta-learning, and inverse problems among other related areas.

Overall, this workshop will provide an excellent platform for researchers and practitioners to showcase their recent work, exchange ideas, and collaborate on future projects. It will feature keynote talks from prominent researchers in the field, as well as contributed talks and posters. Additionally, there will be ample time for discussions and networking during poster sessions, enabling participants to engage with each other. We believe that this workshop will contribute significantly to the advancement of bilevel optimization and its practical applications.

Call for papers

We solicit 4 pages paper contributing novel research ideas at the intersection of bilevel optimization algorithms development and analysis, automatic differentiation and practical use of bilevel optimization, either with theoretically grounded approach or efficient domain specific heuristics. The following themes are of particular relevance:

  • Design of bilevel algorithms,
  • Automatic differentiation,
  • Minimax optimization,
  • Stochastic bilevel optimization,
  • Distributed bilevel optimization,
  • Federated bilevel optimization,
  • Bilevel reinforcement learning,
  • Deep equilibrium networks,
  • Data augmentation,
  • Neural Architecture search,
  • Hyperparameters tuning,
  • Meta-learning,
  • Inverse Problems.

We are also interested in submissions contributing comparison of existing bilevel algorithms or implementing reliably existing approaches in practical libraries.
